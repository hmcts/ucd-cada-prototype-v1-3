Support you need during your case

Case number #1636575673018538

If you need support during the case, you can ask the court to make certain arrangements for you.

You can ask for support while attending hearings, and when you are communicating with the court.

Ask for documents to be provided in another format

Request formats such as braille or large print.

You can request:

  • documents in a specified colour
  • documents in easy read or large print format
  • braille documents
  • audio translation of documents
  • documents to be read aloud

Ask for support with completing forms

Request more context or assistance when filling in forms.

You can request:

  • guidance on how to complete forms
  • support with filling in the forms

Ask for adjustments to travel to and move around the court building

Request accommodations such as help using a lift.

You can request:

  • step free and wheelchair access
  • use of a wheelchair at the venue
  • a parking space close to the venue
  • use of an accessible toilet
  • help with using a lift
  • A different type of chair
  • someone to guide you around the building

Ask to have a source of support with you during a hearing

Request to bring another person with you, such as a family member.

You can request:

  • a support worker
  • a friend or family member
  • an assistance or guide dog
  • a therapy animal

Ask for something to make you feel more comfortable during the hearing

Request more space or privacy during the proceedings.

You can ask for:

  • appropriate lighting
  • regular breaks
  • space to be able to get up and move around
  • a private waiting area

Ask for a specific type of hearing

Request for a hearing to be in person, or by video or phone.

You can ask for:

  • an in person hearing
  • a video hearing
  • a phone hearing

Ask for help communicating and understanding during your case

Request help with having your say, and understanding the proceedings.

You can ask for:

  • extra time to think and explain yourself
  • an intermediary
  • a visit to the court building before the hearing
  • an explanation of the court, and who will be in the room during the hearing

You can also ask the court for:

  • a room fitted with a hearing enhancement system, such as an induction loop or an infrared receiver
  • a speech to text reporter (a palantypist)
  • a lip speaker, or a sign language interpreter

Ask for the court to consider your language requirements

Request that the court provides a language interpreter.

You can ask for:

  • documents in Welsh
  • documents translated to another language
  • a language interpreter

Give details of your specific language requirements.

Give details of what you need the court to do

Explain what support you will need before and during the court hearing.
